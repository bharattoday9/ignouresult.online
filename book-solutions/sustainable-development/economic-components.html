Write a short note on the economic components of sustainable development

12 Dec, 2022 | Written by Naman Kumar

The economic components of sustainable development include efforts to promote economic growth, reduce poverty and inequality, and ensure the sustainable use of natural resources.

In the econommic context, sustainable development is an approach to economic development that seeks to meet the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. It is a process that takes into account the social, economic and environmental aspects of development, and promotes the integration of these three components. This approach is based on principles of equity, efficiency and sustainability, which are necessary for long-term economic growth and prosperity.

In order to achieve sustainable development, it is important to understand the economic components that are involved. These components include:

  • Investment: Investment is the process of committing resources in order to generate economic returns. These returns can be in the form of increased profits, increased jobs, increased output, increased legal protection, and increased access to resources. A sustainable economy requires investments in renewable energy, green technology, and other initiatives that are environmentally friendly.
  • Trade: Trade is the exchange of goods and services between two or more parties. It is an important part of a sustainable economy and is essential for economic growth. Trade increases the efficiency of resource allocation and creates new opportunities for economic growth.
  • Taxation: Taxation is the process of levying taxes on individuals and businesses in order to generate revenue for the government. It is an important part of a sustainable economy, as it helps to fund social programs, infrastructure, and other public services.
  • Financial Markets: Financial markets are the systems that facilitate the exchange of money and other financial instruments. They are an important part of a sustainable economy, as they enable businesses to access capital and investors to access returns.
  • Regulatory Framework: A regulatory framework is a set of rules and regulations that govern the operations of businesses, individuals, and other entities. It is an important part of a sustainable economy, as it helps to ensure that businesses and individuals are operating in an efficient and ethical manner.
  • Innovation: Innovation is the process of developing new products, services, and technologies. It is an important part of a sustainable economy, as it provides businesses with a competitive advantage and helps to drive economic growth.
  • Labor: Labor is the process of employing workers to produce goods and services. It is an important part of a sustainable economy, as it creates jobs and ensures that there is a sufficient supply of labor to meet the needs of businesses.
  • Education: Education is the process of providing individuals with the knowledge and skills necessary to participate in the economy. It is an important part of a sustainable economy, as it helps to ensure that individuals have the skills necessary to contribute to economic growth.
  • Resource Utilization: Resource utilization is the process of using resources in a sustainable manner. It is an important part of a sustainable economy, as it helps to ensure that resources are being used efficiently and that natural resources are not being depleted at an unsustainable rate.

These are the economic components of sustainable development. They are important for long-term economic growth, as they help to ensure that businesses are operating in an efficient and ethical manner, and that individuals have the necessary skills and resources to participate in the economy.

Additional Information from IGNOU SLM (Self Learning Materials)

After the second part of the 20th century, when the majority of countries had gained independence from imperial powers like the UK, Japan, Spain, and Germany, development became one of their main concerns. Around the world, economic growth was a popular policy priority, and development economics was born as a new academic field as a result. It mainly concentrated on the political, social, and economic problems that were met during the development process. Most nations have improved their Gross Domestic Product, sometimes known as GDP, and their Human Development Index globally (HDI). However, Africa has also experienced some unfavourable growth. The issues are with:

  • the social systems that are already in place
  • the widening wealth disparities, the damaging effects of development on the environment

Environmental deterioration, forced migration, and the destruction of cultural and traditional legacy are only a few of the significant costs associated with development. The substantial social and environmental costs have offset the positive economic effects. As more people become aware of these negative repercussions of development, there have been various protests worldwide. Governments and businesses have been under pressure, which has caused them to reconsider strategies and policies that can promote sustainable development.

EXPONENTIAL ERA

The 21st century's environmental issues are not limited to climate change. Due to the vast and exponential growth in global population, the next 30 to 40 years will have to deal with even more issues. Lack of water, access to food, and poverty would cause crises in the loss of species, ecosystems, and ocean health. The finance industry is one area of the 21st century that has seen fast transformation. The international economy might experience a boom or a freefall depending on the tiniest incident in one region of the world. The frequent volatility of oil prices causes economic instability.

The society will soon be impacted by this as well. We find it astonishing that so many people live in poverty and are unable to afford even one square meal each day. Attempts to reverse the trend have been unsuccessful. The stark and escalating disparity between the rich and the poor is making the fight against poverty worse.

Because the impoverished cannot find sustainable means of subsistence, poverty has an equal impact on the social and environmental components. Because of their dependence on natural resources, such as fish and wood for fire, the world is under increased stress.

Additionally, the issues of terrorism, unheard-of street violence, the AIDS epidemic, food security, and nuclear proliferation have massively exacerbated the issue.

ROLE OF INDUSTRY

Large private industries have the potential to play a major role in sustainable development through using their resources, technology, finance and influence. Additionally, they can provide more sustainable options for consumers, and use Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives to be both profitable and beneficial to ecosystems.

ECONOMIC SUSTAINABLE

Economic sustainability is the maximisation of welfare over time from an economic perspective. It requires the production of goods and services to maintain manageable levels of debt and avoid sectoral imbalances. Human welfare is comprised of basic needs such as food, housing, transportation, health, and education. Despite its complexities, economic sustainability can be measured through a single-dimensional indicator.

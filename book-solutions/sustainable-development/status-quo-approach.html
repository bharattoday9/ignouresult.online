Discuss in detail the Status Quo approach to sustainable development

13 Dec, 2022 | Written by Naman Kumar

This question has been asked in IGNOU Check Your Progress of Unit 3 of BPAG-174 book. We have answered the question below.

I can bet you know already by now that Sustainable development is an approach to economic and social development that strives to meet current needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

The status quo approach to sustainable development is a way of maintaining existing economic and social systems while attempting to reduce their negative environmental impacts which seeks to identify and reduce the most immediate threats to sustainability, such as climate change and resource depletion, while allowing for more gradual changes in the development trajectory.

This approach recognises the need for immediate action and hence seeks to reduce the current environmental footprint of economic and social activities, while also allowing for incremental changes over time. The importance of maintaining existing systems and avoiding drastic changes that could disrupt existing infrastructure or lead to unforeseen consequences along with the acknowledgement that change must be gradual, in order to avoid disrupting the current economic and social order are the two key areas of the status quo approach to sustainable development.

One of the key components of the status quo approach to sustainable development is the idea of “no net loss”. This means that any activity that has an environmental impact should be offset by an equivalent activity that has an equal or greater benefit to the environment.

So, we can say that the "Status Quo Approach" helps balance the costs and benefits of any given activity and ensure that the overall result is a net benefit to the environment other than emphasizing the importance of collaboration and public-private partnerships. It recognizes that public and private institutions have different roles and responsibilities, and that both must work together to achieve sustainable development goals. thereby calling for the involvement of all stakeholders, including governments, corporations, civil society, and citizens.

Academicians have focuses on the econominc efficiency, when they talk about the status quo approach, besides recognizes that economic growth is necessary for development, but that it must be managed in a way that is sustainable. They call for the use of resources responsibly and the implementation of policies that promote economic growth without sacrificing the environment.

Finally, it can be said that the status quo approach to sustainable development emphasizes the importance of monitoring and evaluation involving a tracking progress and assessing the impact of any given activity on the environment and ensuring that progress is being made and that any potential risks are being properly addressed.

Notes on "Status Quo Approach" for Examination

  • Since there are no issues that are insurmountable, those who favour the status quo approach contend that no fundamental adjustments are necessary.
  • Although adjustments can be made to bring about improvements, there is no pressing need to alter power structures or decision-making processes (Hopwood, et al., 2005). This is based on the fact that people generally resist change and prefer to stick with what they are familiar with.
  • The lock-in stabilisation mechanisms built into the current systems are another element that supports the status quo.
  • Due to the inertia caused by the expanding advantages of a continued activity, choosing between alternatives becomes challenging. The underlying principles of this strategy are:
    • More development will result in SD and will support the governments' choice to lessen the progressive character of taxation.
    • Sustainability is fueled by business.
    • Increased information, shifting values, new technologies, and innovations are the greatest ways to attain SD.
    • By implementing adjustments to taxation and subsidies and acting on their knowledge, markets will generate goods and services using sustainable methods.
    • Governments' diminished influence will result in the rise of green capitalists, who will engage in corporate citizenship and moral business practises to achieve SD.
    • Reaching sustainability will be made easier by improving good governance on the one hand and reducing corruption on the other.
    • Achieving SD can be facilitated by strategies like EIA (Environmental Impact Assessment), EMAS (EcoManagement and Audit System), BATNEEC (Best Available Techniques not Entailing Excessive Costs), and Cost Benefit Analysis (CBA).
  • Because they view technology as a replacement for nature, proponents of the Status Quo approach are not well known for having a strong commitment to environmental sustainability. Although the World Bank has recognised this strategy as a means of reducing poverty, it makes no mention of a significant change in policy toward SD.

Conclusion

THE status quo approach to sustainable development is an approach that seeks to maintain existing economic and social systems while reducing their environmental impacts; it emphasizes the importance of collaboration, economic efficiency, and monitoring and evaluation, further seeking to reduce the current environmental footprint of economic and social activities, while allowing for incremental changes over time.

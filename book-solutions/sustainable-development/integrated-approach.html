Explain the Integrated approach to sustainable development

13 Dec, 2022 | Written by Naman Kumar

This question is asked in the Check Your Progress 2 section of BPAG-174 study material of IGNOU. I have attached an answer below.

In this answer, I am going to explain how the Integrated Approach to Sustainable Development is a holistic, interconnected approach to addressing the world’s growing environmental, social, and economic challenges. For now, just keep in mind that this approach recognizes the need for a comprehensive, systematic, and balanced approach to achieving sustainable development. It recognizes the interconnectedness of all aspects of life, and the need for a collaborative effort to promote sustainable development. In this article, we will discuss the concept of an integrated approach to sustainable development and its various components.

Defining the Integrated Approach to Sustainable Development

The Integrated Approach to Sustainable Development (IASD) is a comprehensive approach to sustainable development that takes into account the interrelationships between the economic, social, and environmental aspects of development. This approach is based on the notion that all components of the development process must be taken into account in order to achieve a truly sustainable society. The IASD seeks to promote sustainability in all aspects of development, including economic, social, and environmental.

Main Components of the Integrated Approach to Sustainable Development (IASD)

The IASD is composed of four main components:

  • Holistic Thinking: a holistic approach to sustainable development, taking into account the interconnectedness of all parts of the development process.
  • Systematic Planning: the need for a comprehensive and systematic planning process that takes into account the needs of all stakeholders.
  • Collaborative Effort: the need for a collaborative effort among all stakeholders to promote sustainable development.
  • Balanced Solutions: importance of balanced solutions that address the economic, social, and environmental aspects of development.

Benefits of Integrated Approach to Sustainable Development (IASD)

The IASD offers many benefits to society. For an instance, consider the following benefits:

  • It acts as an approach to tackling environmental, social, and economic challenges.
  • IASD helps in a better understanding of the interconnectedness of all aspects of development.
  • It supports better collaborative effort between stakeholders to promote sustainable development.
  • A stronger focus on long-term, balanced solutions that address economic, social, and environmental concerns.
  • A better understanding of the needs of all stakeholders.
  • And a more efficient and effective planning process.

Details from The SLM, IGNOU

The design and adoption of SD strategies and plans that combine environmental, social, and economic components and recognise their interdependencies are referred to as taking an integrated systems approach. Because countries primarily focused on economic growth and poverty reduction, the UN Department of Economic and Social Affairs (UN-DESA) claimed in its released Report that there was a lack of integration, inclusion, and coherence.

Instead of addressing complex societal concerns related to the environment and sustainable development, planning techniques operate in silos. Governments must take into account the connections between systems and the subsystems of those systems, such as water, energy, and ecosystems, as well as their future effects, such as climate change.

Additionally, it stated that because the issues are more systemic and complex in nature, typical sector-based techniques and tools are inappropriate for this use. This is the rationale for the significance of an Integrated Systems approach (ISA) in the formulation of SD planning and strategy (UN, 2015).

Although some academics and strategists have described the transition method as a separate strategy, it is actually a component of the ISA. It will be challenging to go any closer to the SDG Agenda 2030 without a change from the existing approaches. The UN has outlined the following areas for transition:

  • Supportive political system;
  • Access to information;
  • Strong UN system;
  • Consistent coordination;
  • Effective planning agencies;
  • Capacity-building for media and civil society;
  • Membership in multi-lateral agencies; and
  • Education in schools.

Issues in transition to an integrated approach

Now that you undertand the above mentioned concepts related to the integrated approach to sustainable development, let us discuss some of the issues that are faced in the transition to an integrated approach. Note that these issues are not so easy to overcome, neither are they easy to solve. However, they are important to understand in order to understand the challenges that are faced in the transition to an integrated approach.

  • Lack of political will: The lack of political will is one of the biggest issues that are faced in the transition to an integrated approach because the political will is required to make the transition to an integrated approach. If you remember that I previously told that these issues are not so easy to overcome, I want you to think for a moment why it is so w.r.t "lack of political will". The answer is simpler than you think. First of all, you cannot control the wills of others (i.e. our political leaders). And if we cannot do so, how is it going to be easy to make a transition to an integrated approach? You decide.
  • Deficient participation of stakeholders;
  • Corruption;
  • Shocks and insecurity;
  • Lack of finance and debts;
  • Instability and internal/external conflict;
  • Gaps in knowledge;
  • Resistance to change;
  • Lack of transparency;
  • Recognition of civil society;
  • Lack of database; and
  • Youth employment.

I haven't explained majority of these issues because these points are straigh forward and self-explanatory. I don't need to waste time on explaining these simple things. Just follow the same point regarding the "lack of political will" and you will understand the rest of the points as well.

International organisations and governments must take into account the aforementioned barriers to the transition to SD before developing and implementing SDG-related policies. A systems approach is necessary due to the adverse effects of operating in silos, or independently, such as when promoting economic growth without taking social and environmental factors into account.

Importance of Integrated Systems Approach (ISA) or Integrated Approach to Sustainable Development (IASD)

Now I am going to elaborate upon the very importance of ISA, so I demand your full attention.

The ISA is the ideal method for putting SD into practise for the following reasons:

  1. HOLISTIC: The human habitat is a component of the land, climate, and water components that make up the earth's life-support system. Given that climate change is wreaking havoc on society and the economy, it is necessary to consider the eco-chain holistically. For instance, famines and diseases brought on by floods or droughts have a negative impact on civilization as a whole. Social unfairness results from the rich getting richer and the poor getting poorer.
  2. LOCAL CONSIDERATIONS: A single policy cannot effectively execute the SDGs because each country or region's physical location presents unique challenges. It is crucial to know information about the location in terms of its geography, history, cultural standards, etc. Global effect, however, cannot be achieved by localised individual efforts.
  3. SYNERGISM: Synergism refers to the joint efforts of two or more agencies working on multiple SDGs for improved ISA outcomes. If many SDGs are simultaneously sought for achievement, costs may be reduced. According to a research by the European Union, enacting comprehensive climate legislation may prevent the direct costs of environmental degradation and health care from rising by $50 billion year (Future Earth, 2019).

Conclusion

The Integrated Approach to Sustainable Development offers a comprehensive, holistic, and collaborative approach to addressing the world’s growing environmental, social, and economic challenges. This approach recognizes the need for a comprehensive, systematic, and balanced approach to achieving sustainable development. It recognizes the interconnectedness of all aspects of life, and the need for a collaborative effort to promote sustainable development.

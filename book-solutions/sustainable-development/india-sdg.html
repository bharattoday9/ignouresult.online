Examine India’s response to the implementation of Sustainable Development Goals

12 Dec, 2022 | Written by Naman Kumar

In this answer I will be explaining how India has adobted a holistic approach towards the attainment of SDGs.

A comprehensive set of Sustainable Development Goals has been developed, addressing every area of the present generation's environmental, social, and economic demands without compromising the future of the following generations. Consequently, it is a multinational initiative with a multigenerational and multicultural focus.

It should be a known fact that India is one of the signatories of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development and has demonstrated its commitment to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) by adopting several measures. The Indian government has been actively involved in the process of implementing the SDGs since their adoption and has adopted a number of initiatives to ensure their successful implementation. In this article I will examine India’s response to the implementation of the SDGs.

Background of SDG Implementation in India

Now, I am going to explain the background of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) implementation in India.

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development was adopted by all United Nations Member States in September 2015. The Agenda is composed of 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) which are designed to end poverty, protect the planet and ensure that all people enjoy peace and prosperity. India is committed to the 2030 Agenda and has taken several steps to ensure the successful implementation of the SDGs.

India’s response to the implementation of Sustainable Development Goals include the following key pointers:

  • India has launched the Sustainable Development Goals India Index which provides an annual assessment of the progress made by India in achieving the SDGs. The index uses a comprehensive set of indicators to measure the progress of India in achieving the SDGs.
  • A National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC) which sets out the country’s commitment to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and promote adaptation to climate change has been adobted as well. Kindly note down that the NAPCC includes a number of initiatives to reduce emissions and promote sustainability.
  • Above all, India has also adopted a National Clean Air Programme (NCAP) to reduce air pollution. The NCAP includes a number of measures such as improved fuel efficiency, increased use of renewable energy, and better management of industrial emissions.
  • National Waste Management Policy got adobted by India setting out a number of initiatives to reduce waste and promote resource efficiency. This policy includes measures such as improved waste collection and disposal systems, increased recycling and reuse, and better management of hazardous waste.
  • A number of initiatives has been launched by India to promote renewable energy and reduce the country’s reliance on fossil fuels. These initiatives include the Jawaharlal Nehru National Solar Mission, the National Wind Energy Mission, and the National Biofuel Policy.
  • To promote sustainable agriculture and reduce the country’s reliance on chemical fertilizers and pesticides initiatives such as the National Rainfed Area Development Programme and the Soil Health Card Scheme have been created.
  • Lots of initiatives to promote sustainable water managementincluding the National Water Mission and the National Water Quality Monitoring Programme have been implemented as well.
  • To promote gender equality and empower women, initiatives like Beti Bachao Beti Padhao scheme and the Shaadi Bhagti scheme are there.

2023 Agenda for Sustainable Development

The integrated approach, which combines the three components into its strategy for concurrent efforts to take shape, is the best option for achieving the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. The main goal of creating an agenda to accomplish SD is to create a global ranking system for the nations working to meet the 169 targets and 17 goals. By adopting a holistic approach—also known as an integrated or Triple Bottom Line approach—toward the SDGs, India has also linked itself with them.

India has been able to eliminate poverty, raise educational standards, aggressively expand the capabilities of its essential infrastructure, protect its rich biodiversity, and create partnerships for sustainable development.

According to NITI Aayog (2021) following developments have been made in India:

  • Since October 2014, more than 10 crore domestic toilets have been produced. Over 99 percent of our homes and all of our villages have electricity.
  • Between evaluations in 2017 and 2019, our forest cover has grown. The technical assistance initiatives of India are available in 160 nations worldwide.
  • In the previous five years, we have climbed 79 spots in the World Bank's list of nations for ease of doing business, going from 142 in 2014 to 63 in 2019.
  • India and France helped brought together nations that are well-positioned to make significant investments in solar energy to join the International Solar Alliance.

Conclusion:

India has demonstrated its commitment to the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development by adopting a number of initiatives to ensure the successful implementation of the SDGs. India has adopted a number of initiatives to promote renewable energy, reduce air and water pollution, promote sustainable agriculture, and empower women. India has also launched a Sustainable Development Goals India Index to track its progress in achieving the SDGs.

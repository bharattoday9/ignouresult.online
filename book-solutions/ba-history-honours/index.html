Answers to BA History Honours IGNOU Book Questions

12 Dec, 2022 | Written by Naman Kumar

Find answers to the questions asked in the BA History Honours IGNOU Book. The answers are provided by our experts and are 100% correct.

The Bachelor of Arts (History Hons) program offered by IGNOU is an undergraduate program designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the history of India and the world. This program offers a comprehensive view of the past and the present through an in-depth study of the human experience. It covers topics such as the historical development of Indian and world civilizations, the development of different political systems, the spread of religions, the rise and fall of empires, and the impact of modernity on society.

The program is divided into 6 semesters, with each semester having its own specific learning objectives. The first semester focuses on the study of Indian and World History from the ancient period to the 19th century. Students learn about the various aspects of history, including political, social, economic, and cultural aspects. In the second semester, the focus shifts to the 20th century, with students learning about the emergence of new nations, the spread of imperialism, and the impact of World War I and II.

In the third semester, the subject of international relations and diplomacy is studied in detail. This semester also focuses on the study of contemporary international events, such as the Cold War, the Arab-Israeli Conflict, the fall of the Soviet Union, and the rise of globalization. The fourth semester examines the history of India and the world since the end of the 20th century. Students learn about the rise of democracy, the emergence of new technologies, and the changing dynamics of international relations.

Overall, this program offers students a comprehensive understanding of the history of India and the world, and provides them with the knowledge and skills to analyze and interpret historical events. It also helps them to understand the current global political and social developments.

BHIC-106: The Rise of the Modern West 1

BHIC-106 (Rise of the Early Modern West) is a comprehensive study of the evolution of the modern West from the middle of the 15th century. This course examines the social, economic, political, and cultural changes that took place in Europe and North America during this period. It specifically looks at the contribution of the Renaissance, Reformation, and Enlightenment to the growth of Western civilization. The course also covers the development of the welfare state, industrial revolution, and the emergence of nation-states. In addition, it focuses on the scientific and technological advances that led to increased production and international trade. Furthermore, the course will explore the impact of imperialism, colonialism, and mercantilism on the world.
